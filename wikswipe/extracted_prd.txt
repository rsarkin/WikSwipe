1. Product overview
Wikswipe is a mobile-first web app that turns Wikipedia into an addictive, swipeable knowledge feed. Users discover random Wikipedia articles presented as clean cards with a thumbnail, domain tag, and AI-condensed 3-sentence summary. Swipe right to save, swipe left to skip. The more you swipe, the smarter the feed gets.

One-liner
"TikTok for knowledge — powered by Wikipedia."

Problem
Wikipedia has 6.7 million articles but feels like a textbook — nobody browses it for fun.
Discovery is broken — you only land on Wikipedia when Googling something specific.
Knowledge consumption is passive — no feedback loop, no personalisation, no shareability.

Solution
Swipe-based discovery makes Wikipedia browsable and fun.
AI summarisation strips each article to the 3 most interesting sentences.
A sub-topic affinity model learns not just your domain (History) but your micro-interests (WW2, Vikings) vs what you skip (Renaissance).
A story builder lets you export any fact as a shareable vertical card — Spotify Wrapped meets Wikipedia.

3. User personas
Primary — The curious nerd
18–26 years old, college student or early career.
Consumes podcasts, YouTube deep dives, Reddit rabbit holes.
Wants to feel smarter without committing to a full Wikipedia article.
Shares interesting facts on Instagram Stories and WhatsApp.

Secondary — The knowledge flexer
Uses Wikswipe to collect talking points for conversations.
Regularly shares story cards to position themselves as the “smart friend”.
Cares about niche topics — not just broad domains.

4. Feature list
Phase 1 — Core swipe UI + Wikipedia feed
Target: 2–3 weeks. MVP that gets the core loop working with real data.


Phase 2 — Interest onboarding + AI summarisation
Target: 1–2 weeks. Makes the feed feel personalised from day one.


Phase 3 — Sub-topic affinity model (the main twist)
Target: 2–3 weeks. The core differentiator — learns micro-interests, not just broad domains.

The key insight: “History” is not one interest. A user who likes WW1, WW2, and Vikings but consistently skips Renaissance art has a specific taste profile. The affinity model tracks this at the Wikipedia category level.

Scoring rules
Right swipe → +1.0 to all Wikipedia categories of that article
Left swipe → −0.5 (soft penalty, not a hard block)
Immediate skip (< 1 second dwell) → −1.0 (strong penalty)
Wikipedia redirect (tap to read) → +2.0 (strongest positive signal)


Phase 4 — Story builder + sharing
Target: 1–2 weeks. Virality engine — turns any article into a shareable vertical card.

6. API reference
Wikipedia REST API
Base URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1


Claude Haiku API (Phase 2)
Model: claude-haiku-4-5-20251001  |  Max tokens: 200  |  Called per card load via edge function.

Summarisation prompt
Summarise this Wikipedia article in exactly 3 sentences. Sentence 1: what it is. Sentence 2: why it matters historically or scientifically. Sentence 3: one surprising or counterintuitive fact. Be concise. No bullet points. Article: {extract}

Scoring algorithm
score(article) = Σ (category_weight[cat] × affinity[cat])
                for each cat in article.categories

12. Open questions
